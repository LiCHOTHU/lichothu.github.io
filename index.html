Bio

I am a Ph.D. student in Computer Science at Georgia Institute of Technology, advised by Professor Animesh Garg. My research interests lie in Robotics, Computer Vision, and Reinforcement Learning. I have extensive experience in robotic manipulation tasks, including developing MPM particle systems in PhysX and working with cutting-edge technologies like IsaacSim and IsaacGym.

Prior to joining Georgia Tech, I transferred to the University of Toronto in 2017 after beginning my undergraduate studies at Tsinghua University in Beijing, where I studied physics. I later completed my Master of Science in Computer Science at the University of Toronto, specializing in robotics.

In addition to my academic pursuits, I have completed internships at Nvidia where I worked on robotics simulation, and have published several papers in leading robotics and AI conferences.
